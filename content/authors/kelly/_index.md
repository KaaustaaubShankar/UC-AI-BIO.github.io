Professor Kelly Cohen is the Brian H. Rowe Endowed Chair in Aerospace Engineering at the University of Cincinnati and Director of the AI Bio Lab at UC Digital Futures. His research focuses on explainable and responsible artificial intelligence, with emphasis on fuzzy-logic-based systems for safety-critical applications including aerospace autonomy, advanced mobility, and human-AI teaming.

Over more than three decades, Dr. Cohen has pioneered genetic fuzzy systems and certifiable AI architectures designed to improve transparency, robustness, and operational trust in intelligent systems. His work spans aerospace, robotics, biomedical applications, and decision-support systems, often in collaboration with government and industry partners including NASA, NSF, and the U.S. Department of Defense.

Dr. Cohen leads interdisciplinary research at the AI Bio Lab focused on trustworthy AI, advanced autonomy, and human performance augmentation, aiming to enable safe deployment of AI in complex real-world environments.
